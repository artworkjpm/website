When I first bought my cheap Ridgeback Velocity hybrid bike from Evans in Clerkenwell, London, back in 2006, I had no idea it was the beginning of love affair that would develop into a long term happy marriage.

My plan was to buy a bike to get around London quicker, mostly on the weekends as I was living centrally and could walk to work. There had been a slow burning trend towards cycling in London, in some parts due to the tragic 7/7 events in 2005. Bike sales that summer shot up after the incident for two reasons:

  1. Tubes and Buses were down for at least two weeks
  2. People were worried they would be murdered!

Friends I knew in London told me it was insane to see so many cyclists around that summer (I was working on-board a cruise ship as a Photographer in the Mediterranean when 7/7 happened).

The London Transport system soon reopened and the mass cycling started to diminish, by the winter time is was virtually back to normal.

However there was an undercurrent of a cycling craze. After I bought my bike, I had to wait a day for them get it ready. I spent a lot of money on accessories, it was crazy. I needed:

  • A lock
  • Lights
  • Mudguards
  • Helmet
  • Gloves
  • Mini tools
  • Pump
  • Puncture repair kit
  • And so on.....

What was a cheap bike ended up being quite pricey. Anyway the next day the bike was ready, I saw it shining in the shop, noticeably taller than other bikes because I'm 6'5. I took the bike outside, hopped on and off I went. And then.....wow.....

Suddenly I am on the road with cars, buses, motorbikes, I hadn't driven a car for years and suddenly I am classed as a vehicle. I have to stop at red lights and indicate by using my arms - I could have an accident! However suddenly I am in Holborn Circus - within 1 minute!

I carry on and the next minute I'm in St Pauls. Cycling is fast, way faster than buses and taxis, plus I'm reliving my childhood!

It doesn't take long to cycle to Liverpool Street, I turn right and head to Shoreditch, stopping at Hoxton Square to catch my breath and to calm the adrenaline that is pumping in my body. I can see some hipsters drinking and smoking outside a nice looking bar.

Its spring time around 6pm, there is still plenty of sunlight. I am outside, getting exercise on a vehicle faster than cars in a bustling city - I am already hooked on cycling
